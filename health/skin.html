01
Wash face/scalp 5–6x per week — not less
Contrary to popular advice, participants who washed daily scored better on every skin and scalp outcome than those washing once a week. Infrequent washing allows sebum and bacteria to accumulate.
[Prinyani et al., 2022]
02
High-pH cleansers damage the skin barrier and increase breakouts
Of 100+ commercial shampoos tested, only 1/3 had a pH ≤5.5. Higher pH forces the cuticle open, leading to tangling, breakage, and frizz on hair — and disrupts the acid mantle on skin.
[Yavuz Uçar et al., 2020]
03
Sodium is the primary cause of facial bloating — most is hidden in processed food
The body holds water to maintain serum osmolality. Two slices of white bread alone contain 300–450mg sodium. Reducing processed food intake visibly reduces facial puffiness within days.
[American Heart Association]
04
Alcohol dehydrates and simultaneously causes facial puffiness
Even a single dose of ethanol significantly increased transepidermal water loss. Simultaneously, vasodilation causes fluid to leak into surrounding tissue — producing the deflated-yet-puffy appearance after a night out.
[Jacobi et al., 2018]
05
Lymphatic drainage direction determines gua sha effectiveness
The lymphatic system has no pump — it relies on movement and manual stimulation toward drainage nodes near the ears and neck. Moving a tool in the wrong direction merely displaces fluid without draining it.
[Annetta et al., 2021]
06
Late-night carbs cause morning puffiness — each gram of glycogen holds 3g water
Lying horizontal for 6–8 hours means gravity can't assist drainage. Fluid from glycogen storage accumulates in the midface, under-eyes, and jawline overnight.
[Fernandez et al., 2010]
07
Lip color contrast directly affects perceived health and sex typicality
Research shows contrast between lip color and surrounding skin is a key health signal. Dry or cracked lips reduce this contrast and make the entire face look duller regardless of other grooming.
[Stephen & McKeegan, 2010]
