Evidence basis
150 min/week moderate cardio target — WHO Global Action Plan for Physical Activity 2018; AHA guidelines. Moderate = Zone 2–3, RPE 4–6/10.
RHR reduction from aerobic training — meta-analysis of 191 studies (Reimers et al. 2018, J. Clin. Med.): endurance training reduces RHR ~5–8 bpm; effect larger when baseline is elevated.
Amitriptyline raises RHR +10–15 bpm — Brosen & Gram 1992 (Psychopharmacology): mean increase 78→94 bpm at 150mg/day.
Chronotropic incompetence on TCAs — JACC Case Reports 2022: cyclist lost 20 bpm off peak HR on desipramine; resolved on cessation.
QTc 453 ms (2024) — mildly abnormal for males (threshold 450 ms). Amitriptyline adds ~8–10 ms. Dangerous threshold: 500+ ms. Source: European Society of Cardiology guidelines.
Aerobic exercise and prediabetes — aerobic training is one of the most effective interventions for high-normal fasting glucose (ADA 2024 Standards of Care).
