Hairstyle — Know Your Four Features
Reference · by impact
01
Wash 5–6x per week — not once or twice as commonly advised
Controlled trial found daily washers scored better on every scalp and hair condition outcome than weekly washers. Infrequent washing creates an unfavorable scalp environment via sebum and dead cell buildup.
[Prinyani et al., 2022]
02
140°C is the critical heat threshold — above it, damage is permanent
Research identified 140°C as where reversible changes become permanent bond breakage. Most flat irons and curlers operate at 150–230°C. Heat protectants only offset ~20% of this damage.
[Boris et al., 2021] · [McMullen & Jachowicz, 2021]
03
UV radiation attacks hair keratin — salt water and chlorine compound the damage
UVB causes protein loss; UVA causes color degradation. The combination of chlorine and sunlight in pools was shown to be "much more damaging than each factor separately."
[Sabatier et al., 2011] · [Pires Oliveira et al., 2019]
04
Hairstyle appearance directly affects how attractive the face is perceived
Research confirms that perceived hair attractiveness influences facial attractiveness ratings even when the face itself has not changed — making the right haircut one of the highest-leverage interventions available.
[Tagai et al., 2016]
05
Face shape is too broad a guide — the four specific features determine the right cut
Facial thirds proportion, symmetry, vertical face length, and jaw projection each independently constrain which hairstyle works. Styling based on face shape alone misses most of the relevant information.
[Mogilski & Welling, 2018]
06
Alcohol depletes hair nutrients — damage appears weeks to months later
Hair follicles depend on zinc, biotin, iron, and B vitamins that alcohol depletes. Because hair grows ~½ inch/month, the damage from a drinking period won't be visible for weeks after it occurs.
[Goodman et al., 2019]
